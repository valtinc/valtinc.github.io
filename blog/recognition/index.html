The Power of Recognition Memory

Take a quick look at the six images below.

Now go back and imagine a single word description for each one. Really, do it. Say it out loud, even. You won’t be disappointed.

Now, it may come as a surprise, but humans are freakishly good at recognizing images they’ve seen before. In fact, this is one reason Valt is so secure. We generate a random text-based password for you and then use your innate ability to recognize images to translate that password into a set of images.

How it works

Here’s a simple example. Take a look at the grid of images below. There are 6 columns. Each column has 10 images, one of which is an image from above. Now, without looking back, try to identify the six images in the six columns below.

Did you do it? Not bad, right? And the next time it will already be easier.

Now, any chance you know the first six digits of Pi? Don’t worry if you don’t. Only 19% of people in one survey could come up with 3.14159 when asked. But take a look at the grid again and, moving from the first column to the sixth column, notice the row in which each image appears.

Yeah, you guessed it. The images are in rows corresponding to the first six digits of Pi. But here’s the best part—grids like this can actually represent any six digit number—you just need a new grid and to select the appropriate six images. And here’s the best part—the grid doesn’t tell you anything about which six digits it represents—only you know that because only you know which images are yours.

That’s the key with Valt—because only you know your images your information is secure.
